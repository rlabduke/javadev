1. What is a kinemage?

A kinemage is a "kinetic image," a three-dimensional illustration that lends itself to a variety of subjects. Kinemages were first created as a way of visualizing macromolecular structures, particularly x-ray crystal structures of proteins. Their strength is in selective presentation of information --- they are intended as an alternative to flat, static journal illustrations. Since their creation, kinemages have been extended to a variety of other fields, from the social sciences to education.

The kinemage concept is the brainchild of Drs. David and Jane Richardson at Duke University in Durham, North Carolina, USA. Their website is at http://kinemage.biochem.duke.edu.
